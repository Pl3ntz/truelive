Honest expectations

What delay will you get?

Straight talk: the minimum delay doesn't depend only on the extension. The channel picks the latency class when it goes live:

How the channel streams Physical floor With TrueLive you sit at
Ultra-low latency ~2-5s ~3-4s — broadcast-TV territory
Low latency (esports, most sports streams) ~5-10s ~3-4s — broadcast-TV territory
Normal latency (YouTube's default) ~10-30s the stream's floor (~10s+)

Why can't anyone go lower? On a normal-latency stream, the newest video simply hasn't arrived at your computer yet — not before ~10s — no magic breaks that floor, and anyone promising otherwise is lying. What TrueLive guarantees: you're always at the lowest delay that stream allows. The in-player badge shows your real, measured delay — if it reads 10s on a normal-latency channel, you're already at the minimum.

Under the hood

How it works

There's nothing to configure. Turn on Super Live and it handles the rest:

Measures your real delay

Uses the player's internal ingestion clock to know exactly how many seconds you are behind the live edge — and shows it in a discreet badge, if you want.

Rides the live edge

Part of the "future" video is already downloaded on your computer. TrueLive repositions playback right at that edge — that's why it gets closer than any extension that merely speeds up the video.

Protects itself

It measures your connection's "breathing" and keeps a safe distance. If your internet wobbles, it steps back instantly — playback speed never drops and the video doesn't freeze. Genuinely weak connection? It suspends itself and returns when it's safe.

Install

Install in 1 click

We're on the official Chrome Web Store. No files, no developer mode.

  1. Open TrueLive on the Chrome Web Store and click "Add to Chrome". Google's official store. Updates arrive automatically.
  2. Done! Open a live stream on YouTube, click the puzzle-piece icon at the top of Chrome and pin TrueLive. Click it and choose "Super Live". The in-player badge shows your delay dropping.
  3. Want the newest beta before it lands on the store? Download the latest .zip — it's always the first one on the page — and follow the walkthrough in INSTALL.md.
  1. Easiest path: install from the Chrome Web Store — Edge, Brave and Opera accept Chrome's store just fine. Edge shows a prompt asking to "Allow extensions from other stores". Just accept it.
  2. Prefer manual install (or want the newest beta)? Download TrueLive here — on the release at the top of the page, open Assets and click the first .zip file (its name starts with truelive-).
  3. Double-click the downloaded file to create the "truelive" folder. Don't delete that folder afterwards — the extension lives in it.
  4. Paste into the address bar and press Enter — Edge: edge://extensions · Brave: brave://extensions · Opera: opera://extensions
  5. Turn on "Developer mode" (switch in the page corner).
  6. Click "Load unpacked" and pick the "truelive" folder.
  7. Done! Open a live stream, pin TrueLive and choose "Super Live".
  1. On Firefox, the safe install path is the official store — our version is under Mozilla review. Firefox requires every extension to be signed by Mozilla, even outside the store — that protects you, and we follow the rule.
  2. Once approved, the button here becomes "Add to Firefox" — one click and done.
  3. Don't want to wait? Use the Chrome/Edge/Brave version above meanwhile.
Questions

Quick questions

Will it freeze my video?

No — that's product rule #1. TrueLive measures your connection continuously and keeps a safety reserve that adapts on its own. If your internet wobbles, it steps back instantly (you barely notice) instead of letting the video freeze. If the connection is genuinely bad, it suspends itself for 10 minutes.

Does video quality drop?

No. TrueLive doesn't touch resolution or quality — only the distance between you and the newest point of the stream.

Do you collect my data?

The extension collects zero: no external connections, no sign-up, no tracking. Everything happens in your browser, and the code is open source (GPL-3.0) for anyone to verify. This website only keeps an anonymous, cookie-free visit count (self-hosted Umami).

Why still 3 seconds? Couldn't it be 0?

No — even broadcast TV isn't 0 (it runs ~3-5s from the event to your antenna). The signal must leave the venue, get encoded, reach YouTube and travel to you. Those ~3s are the physical floor of that path — and that's exactly where TrueLive puts you.

Does it work on every live stream?

It works on any YouTube live stream. The minimum delay depends on how the channel broadcasts (see the table above): on low-latency channels you sit at ~3-4s; on normal-latency channels the floor is ~10s+ for everyone — TrueLive guarantees you the minimum from there. Twitch and other platforms are on the roadmap.

Is it safe? Why trust it?

The code is 100% open and auditable on GitHub. The extension asks for a single permission (storing your preferences) and touches nothing outside the YouTube player.
